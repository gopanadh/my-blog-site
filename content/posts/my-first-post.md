I am Gopanadh, an Information Science first year student in APU. I'm writing this blog as part of an assessment on the course Becoming a Reflective Information Scientist.

The first unit named What is information? mainly focuses on data, how it is handled and its representation. First class was about Becoming a Reflective Information Scientist. On this topic, I learnt about data misrepresentation through graphs. The topic wasn't new but I liked the way it portrayed. We had a bar graph with some data where the difference was not very much but it portrayed as a huge difference in the bars. Also went through different types of graphs but the main idea was same - small difference in data or numbers but portrayed as a huge difference. A person taking a quick glance will misunderstand the graph. This shows how the way data portrayed can have impact on the readers. The topic was interesting because it could be used to convince or delude people even though the topic was to give us the idea of correct representation of data. In the next class, I learnt about DIKW model  which is about obtaining wisdom from data - data -> information -> knowledge -> wisdom and its drawbacks. I learnt about how it can be applied in real life situations. In following classes, I learnt about different types of graphical representation and tried a few. One thing that I found challenging about the course was to focus in the class. The class time is from 6:15 - 8:15. By the evening, I would be tired and feeling sleepy and also I'm not a person who can focus on lectures for hours. I always drift to some thoughts. It happens in almost every evening and morning classe, in other courses as well.
