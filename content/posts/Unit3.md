Unit 3 was something new to me. I always thought of failure as something bad but unit 3 beautifully portrayed that failure is natural. I learned a lot about failure especially through the group work. Our group work was kind of a failure. We agreed to split the work so that each person will be writing about specific task. Mine was to work on the case study. I researched a little bit about the incident and when I was about to write, someone else did my part. So I had to work on book summary instead. When I got to know about I had to work on the case study, I thought I would just go through the book. But because of the change in plan, I had to work till late night. Reading for understanding and reading to write summary are entirely different for me, so I took more time for it. 

This experience was totally relatable to the chapter our group got. The chapter was about system thinking and quick fixes. I am a person who procrastinates lot. In the chapter one example was about quick fixes, delaying things and how it can cause negative impact later. System thinking is thinking as a whole system, which address all the problems that might even happen in the future. we only thought more of our self like we will do the exact assigned part and the group work will be complete. But in the end it wasn’t. lots of confusions were going on and all of us had to work together in the end to shape it up. This had made me understand even though a group has capable members, the work can still be a failure. We failed to think as a whole.

The case study was about AT\&T long distance network failure in 1990. Even though AT\&T is famous for testing, they had failures. The case study had helped me to understand there is no such a thing as perfection it is okay to have failures.
